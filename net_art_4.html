The Legal Battle

After the websites publication, 8 US States issued temporary restraining orders or injunctions on the basis of illegal vote trading.

The 2 primary domains for the Vote-Auction platform (voteauction.com and vote-auction.com) were shutdown.

The legal battle resulted in the FBI, NSA as well as Federal Attorney Janet Reno, to investigate whether the case would have any effect on the upcoming presidential elections in the year 2000.

The Vote-Auction case was featured in over 2500 news reports including both national and international networks. It was featured in print, online media, television and radio, with CNN creating a half hour exclusive program titled "Burden of Proof")



In response to all the media attention, UBERMORGEN conducted a series of press-releases to reveal the new location of the website. This generated even more publicity and notoriety for the website. However, the increased spotlight resulted in several server-infrastructure attacks,forcing the website to once again move to a more secure location.
